\begin{abstract}
This paper consolidates a multi-stage research program attacking the
Union-Closed Sets (Frankl) Conjecture through a single structural lens
--- \emph{compatibility geometry} --- together with its cohomological
continuation. We assemble, into one coherent document, two parallel
arcs: a native combinatorial reduction that rewrites Frankl's
conjecture as a sequence of progressively sharper, explicit finitary
inequalities; and a cohomological program that constructs a genuine
$(\Z/2)^n\!\rtimes\!\Sym_n$-equivariant cohomology theory of
intersection-closed families and sets up a two-part contradiction on a
distinguished obstruction class.

The paper is written to be \emph{honest about its own state}, and that
honesty is its main contribution to the program record. \textbf{The
conjecture is not proved here.} The combinatorial arc reduces Frankl to
one named, explicit integer inequality and settles it on a hierarchy of
structured sub-families (recovering the classical small-generator cases
natively). The cohomological arc produces one genuinely unconditional
theorem --- a trace-injectivity / null-homotopy result --- and a
correct \emph{reduction} of the remaining work, but its closing
contradiction depends on a lower-Walsh vanishing statement, here called
$Y_1$, whose previously circulated proof we show to be invalid. We
record three corrections to the ledger: the proof of $Y_1$ conflates
exactness of a trace map with vanishing of cohomology; the auxiliary
``$R2$'' chain-isomorphism advertised as repairing that gap is provably
\emph{false}; and the existing machine-checked encodings of the
load-bearing isotype statements are answer-steered. We isolate the one
genuine open problem the program now rests on --- the
$\walsh{S}$-cohomology of the cofiber of a matched-cylinder inclusion,
a comma-category twist of the trace functor --- and state it precisely
as the recommended next target.
\end{abstract}
