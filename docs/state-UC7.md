## Invariants (reproduce-on-resume)

Load-bearing facts for any future session. UC1–UC6 invariants (`docs/state-UC1.md` … `state-UC6.md`) remain in force; these are the UC7 additions.

- **Setup (carried).** Minimal generator $W$, $|W|=k\ge 3$, $U'=U\setminus W$; fibre $\mathcal R_S$, co-fibre $\mathcal S(R)$ (union-closed on $W$, $\ni W$); $\phi(S)=2|S|-k$; defect retraction $\bar\jmath_R(S)$, deficiency $\delta_R(S)$, $\mathcal D(R)=\mathcal S(R)^\uparrow\setminus\mathcal S(R)$; fibre-ideals $I_{R,S_0}\subseteq 2^{W_0}$, $a_{S_0}=|I_{R,S_0}|$; rooted mass $M(R)=\sum_{S_0}a_{S_0}|S_0|=\sum_{S\in\mathcal S(R)^\uparrow}|\bar\jmath_R(S)|$.
- **Boundary identity (Thm 1.1).** $B(R)=M(R)-\Phi(\mathcal S(R)^\uparrow)$. Equivalent to UC6 Thm 1.4 rearranged. $B(R)\le M(R)$ always (since $\Phi(\mathcal S(R)^\uparrow)\ge 0$, UC4 Cor 1.2). The global lower bound $\sum_R B(R)\ge\sum_R M(R)-\sum_R\Phi(\mathcal S(R))\iff D^{\mathrm{lg}}(W)\le 0$ — Phase-1 lever is the residual itself.
- **Half-level-deficiency class (Thm 1.2).** Every $R\in\mathcal R_W^{\mathrm{lg}}$ has $\mathcal D(R)\subseteq\{|S|\le k/2\}\Rightarrow\Phi(\mathcal D(R))\le 0\Rightarrow$ Frankl. Properly contains UC6 Thm 1.6 (low-rooted shallow) for $k\ge 4$.
- **$k=3$ 1-rich class (Thm 1.3).** $|\mathcal D(R)^{(1)}|\ge|\mathcal D(R)^{(2)}|$ for every $R\in\mathcal R_W^{\mathrm{lg}}\Rightarrow\Phi(\mathcal D(R))\le 0\Rightarrow$ Frankl. Properly contains UC6 Thm 1.6 at $k=3$ — Example 1.4 witness ($\mathcal F=\{W,R,R\cup\{1\},R\cup\{2,3\},U\}$, $R=\{4,5,6\}$ large; $\mathcal S(R)=\{\emptyset,\{1\},\{2,3\},W\}$ union-closed; $a_{\{1\}}=3,|S_0=\{1\}|=1$ so not low-rooted; $|\mathcal D^{(1)}|=|\mathcal D^{(2)}|=2$, 1-rich; $\Phi(\mathcal D)=0$).
- **$W$-balanced class (Thm 2.1).** Every $A\in\mathcal F\setminus\mathcal W$ with $|A\cap W|\ge k/2\Rightarrow\sigma_{\mathrm{rest}}(W)\ge 0$ pointwise.
- **Uniformly $W$-balanced + $\Gamma$-sparse (Thm 2.2).** $\sum_W\sigma_{\mathrm{rest}}(W)\ge 0\ \&\ \Sigma_{\mathcal W}\ge 0\Rightarrow\sum_W\sigma(W)\ge 0\Rightarrow$ Frankl (via I1 identity). Combined Phase-1/Phase-2 closure on the structured intersection.
- **Weighted refinement (Thm 2.3).** $c:\mathcal W\to\mathbb R_{>0}$ with $\sum_W c_W\deg_\Gamma(W)\le\sum_W c_W$ and $\sum_W c_W\sigma_{\mathrm{rest}}(W)\ge 0\Rightarrow\sum_W c_W\sigma(W)\ge 0\Rightarrow$ Frankl.
- **Cumulative-degree refinement (Thm 2.4).** $\sum_{x\in A}\deg_{\mathcal W}(x)\ge kN/2$ for every $A\in\mathcal F\setminus\mathcal W\Rightarrow\sum_W\sigma_{\mathrm{rest}}(W)\ge 0$. Non-pointwise (some $|A\cap W|<k/2$ allowed if compensated).
- **Excess characterization (Thm 3.1).** $T\in X(R,R')=\mathcal S(R\cup R')\setminus(\mathcal S(R)\vee\mathcal S(R'))$ iff [$T\notin\mathcal S(R)^\uparrow$] OR [$T\notin\mathcal S(R')^\uparrow$] OR [$T\in\mathcal S(R)^\uparrow\cap\mathcal S(R')^\uparrow$, $\delta_R(T)\cap\delta_{R'}(T)\ne\emptyset$]. Equivalent: $T\in\mathcal S(R)\vee\mathcal S(R')\iff T=\bar\jmath_R(T)\cup\bar\jmath_{R'}(T)$ (both defined).
- **Up-closure intersection identity (Cor 3.1b).** $(\mathcal S(R)\vee\mathcal S(R'))^\uparrow=\mathcal S(R)^\uparrow\cap\mathcal S(R')^\uparrow$.
- **Graded-equality common-domain maximality (Thm 3.2).** $\mathcal F$ graded-equal $\Rightarrow$ for every $R\in\mathcal R_W$, $\mathcal S(R^*)^\uparrow\subseteq\mathcal S(R)^\uparrow$ (where $R^*=\bigcup\mathcal R_W$), and the common domain $\mathcal S(R)^\uparrow\cap\mathcal S(R^*)^\uparrow=\mathcal S(R^*)^\uparrow$ — UC6 Cor 3.2's deficiency anti-monotonicity holds on the full top-fibre up-closure (no shrinkage from intersection).
- **Cross-$R$ top-fibre isolation (Thm 3.3).** Graded-equality $\Rightarrow\mathcal D(R^*)\subseteq\mathcal D(R)$ for every $R$; so $\Phi(\mathcal D(R))=\Phi(\mathcal D(R^*))+\Phi(\mathcal D(R)\setminus\mathcal D(R^*))$, top-fibre defect = uniform additive piece in every fibre's defect. Residual reduces to signing per-fibre excesses $\Phi(\mathcal D(R)\setminus\mathcal D(R^*))\le 0$, bounded by the structural gap between $\mathcal S(R)^\uparrow$ and $\mathcal S(R^*)^\uparrow$.
- **Fully-signed class (Thm 4.1).** (Half-level-deficient OR 1-rich at $k=3$) ∩ uniformly $W$-balanced ∩ graded-equal ∩ $\Gamma$-sparse $\Rightarrow$ Frankl unconditionally. Non-empty at $k=3$ (Example 4.1: $\mathcal F=\{\{1,2,3\},\{1,2,4\},\{1,2,3,4\}\}$).
- **No-go from union-closure alone (Prop 4.2).** Each of the three structural conditions (half-level-deficiency, uniformly $W$-balanced, graded-equality) admits a union-closure-compatible counterexample. Structural input beyond union-closure required to sign the levers unconditionally.
