## Invariants (reproduce-on-resume)

Load-bearing facts for any future session or for UC4; re-check against the doc before extending. (UC1's invariants in `docs/state-UC1.md` and UC2's in `docs/state-UC2.md` remain in force; these are the UC3 additions.)

- **Setup.** Fix a minimal generator $W\in\mathcal F$, $\lvert W\rvert=m(\mathcal F)=k\ge3$; $U'=U\setminus W$. Each $A\in\mathcal F$ splits $A=S\sqcup R$, $S=A\cap W$, $R=A\setminus W$. Fibre $\mathcal R_S=\{R:S\sqcup R\in\mathcal F\}$, profile $p_S=|\mathcal F^S|=|\mathcal R_S|$. Target functional $\phi(S)=2|S|-k$; target $=\langle p,\phi\rangle=\sum_{x\in W}\beta_x\ge0$.
- **Graded union law (Prop 2.2).** $\mathcal R_S\vee\mathcal R_{S'}\subseteq\mathcal R_{S\cup S'}$ — the complete translation of union-closure into the trace picture. Diagonal case: each $\mathcal R_S$ is union-closed.
- **Universal fibre inclusion (Thm 2.3).** $\mathcal R_S\subseteq\mathcal R_W$ for *every* $S\subseteq W$; hence $p_S\le p_W$. $\mathcal R_W$ is union-closed on $U'$ with $\emptyset\in\mathcal R_W$ — the single ambient fibre. The inclusions are NOT graded ($S\subseteq S'\not\Rightarrow\mathcal R_S\subseteq\mathcal R_{S'}$); they relay only through $S'\in\mathcal F$, and by minimality the only such $S'$ in $2^W$ are $W$ (and $\emptyset$ if $\emptyset\in\mathcal F$).
- **Dual reduction (Thm 2.5).** Co-fibre $\mathcal S(R)=\{S:S\sqcup R\in\mathcal F\}$ is union-closed on $W$ containing the top $W$; $p=\sum_{R\in\mathcal R_W}\mathbf 1_{\mathcal S(R)}$; co-fibres coupled by $\mathcal S(R)\vee\mathcal S(R')\subseteq\mathcal S(R\cup R')$. So the sub-cube Walsh spectrum is the Walsh transform of an unweighted superposition of indicators of union-closed families on $W$.
- **Minimality grading (Prop 2.6).** Intermediate trace $S$, $0<|S|<k$: every $R\in\mathcal R_S$ has $|R|\ge k-|S|>0$. Dually: $0<|R|<k\Rightarrow\emptyset\notin\mathcal S(R)$, and intermediate $S\in\mathcal S(R)\Rightarrow|S|\ge k-|R|$.
- **Target dualized (Prop 3.1).** $\sum_{x\in W}\beta_x=\sum_{R\in\mathcal R_W}\Phi(\mathcal S(R))$, $\Phi(\mathcal G)=\sum_{S\in\mathcal G}(2|S|-k)$. Per-fibre statement is false in isolation (Obs 3.2: $\{\emptyset,\{x\},W\}$ has $\Phi=2-k<0$) — the couplings must be used.
- **Per-fibre sign theorem (Thm 3.3).** $|R|\le\lfloor k/2\rfloor\Rightarrow\Phi(\mathcal S(R))\ge0$; indeed $\ge k$ for $1\le|R|\le\lfloor k/2\rfloor$, and $\in\{0,k\}$ for $R=\emptyset$. The threshold $\lfloor k/2\rfloor$ is exactly where the minimality grading lets a negative-weight trace appear.
- **Large-fibre localization (Cor 3.4).** $\mathcal R_W=\mathcal R_W^{\mathrm{sm}}\sqcup\mathcal R_W^{\mathrm{lg}}$ ($|R|\le\lfloor k/2\rfloor$ vs $>$); the small part contributes $\ge0$; the entire residual difficulty is carried by the large fibres.
- **Sufficient condition (Thm 3.5).** $W^+=\bigcup\{A\in\mathcal F:A\supseteq W\}$; if $|W^+|\le\lfloor3k/2\rfloor$ (equivalently $|W^+\setminus W|\le\lfloor k/2\rfloor$) then $\mathcal R_W^{\mathrm{lg}}=\emptyset$ and some $x\in W$ is abundant. A new native region of the $m(\mathcal F)\ge3$ frontier.
- **Gilmer framing (§4).** The entropy method consumes the level-1 spectrum (the abundances). The UC2 localization is a strict refinement of that input — the graded union law + minimality grading are the coordinate couplings the decoupled per-coordinate relaxation discards. The Sawin ceiling $(3-\sqrt5)/2$ is the optimum of that relaxation; it does not apply to the coupled localized object. UC3 does not prove a bound past the ceiling — it removes the structural reason the method stalls there, and recasts the residual as a finitary exact inequality.
